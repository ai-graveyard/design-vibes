One material. Every layer.

Liquid Glass isn't a coat of paint over the interface — it's what the interface is made of, from the lock screen down to the smallest switch.

Refraction, in real time

Every panel samples the scene behind it, then bends that light through thirty layers of blur and a 180% color lift. Windows feel like lenses, not stickers.

Depth without shadows

Layers separate by optics, not dark halos. A brighter top edge, a softer fill, a thinner rim — your eye reads the whole stack in a single glance.

Alive to what's beneath

Drag a sheet across a sunset photo and watch its tint warm up. Glass saturates the color it covers, so nothing behind it ever turns to mud.
