A teahouse in the northern hills

The beauty of what is left empty.

Three rooms, one kettle, and the long quiet of the valley. We serve tea the slow way — so that the pauses may say what words cannot.

Reserve a seat On emptiness
余白　よはく
間
Ma — the interval

Space is not the absence of things. It is the room they need to breathe.

A bowl is useful because it is hollow. A room is livable because it is unfilled. Between two sounds of the kettle there is a silence, and the tea is mostly made of it. We build our days here around that interval.

Three sittings daily

Each seat, one hour. Six guests, no more.

  • 朝 AsaMorning seat Mist still in the cedars. A pale sencha, steamed rice, and the first light through paper. 06:30 — 07:30
  • 午 HiruNoon seat Roasted hōjicha and a single seasonal sweet, taken facing the dry garden. 12:00 — 13:00
  • 夕 YūDusk seat Thick matcha in winter, thin in summer. The lamps are lit only after the last pour. 17:30 — 18:30
The vessels

Our bowls are old, and some of them are broken. Those are the ones we reach for first.

Each winter a local kiln fires a small batch for the house; each spring we mend what the year has cracked, seam by seam, in lacquer and gold. The repair is not hidden. It is the history of the bowl, written where everyone can read it.

Mended vessels may be purchased once retired from service.
